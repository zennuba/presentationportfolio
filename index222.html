Project: Website Redesign

Client-Based Redesign with UX Evaluation, Testing & Live Pitch

This project involved the improvement of an existing website through a structured UX evaluation. A heuristic analysis and user testing identified key usability issues, which were translated into clear, actionable design improvements.

The final result was a high-fidelity prototype developed in Figma, including a redesigned landing page and selected subpages optimized for user experience. All findings and design decisions were documented in a professional client report, supported by a short promotional video. The project concluded with a live pitch to present the concept and rationale to stakeholders.

Client Concept: Educational Experience

From Research to Prototype - An Iterative UX Process

As part of a client collaboration with Økolariet, this concept was developed to support the exhibition “Den Grønne Trepart” (The Green Tripartite). The goal was to transform complex political and environmental themes into an interactive digital experience aimed at younger audiences. A high-fidelity prototype was designed in Figma, presenting a playful yet informative journey through key decisions shaping Denmark´s green transition.

Design challenge App

Rapid Delivery Concept Based on User Research & Design Challenge

This project explored how to solve the everyday challenge of running out of essential items—like milk—when time is tight. The goal was to design a fast and convenient last-minute shopping solution. Based on user interviews, surveys, and persona development, the concept evolved into a mobile app called FLUKS. It offers ultra-fast local delivery of small, urgent grocery items. The outcome was a hi-fi prototype focused on UX simplicity, speed, and relevance, developed through iterative testing and feedback.

Design challenge: Social Planning App for Kids

A Safe and Fun Way to Make Playdates

This concept focused on developing an intuitive and secure app that empowers children to initiate and manage their own playdates—under light parental guidance. Inspired by challenges shared by parents during user interviews, the goal was to support children’s independence, communication, and social skills in a digital format. The app includes a kid-friendly interface with visual cues, customizable avatars, and voice-supported navigation. Parents have access to a supervision panel to approve or manage connections and schedules. The design process included co-creation workshops with children, low-fi prototyping, and iterative usability testing to ensure accessibility and engagement. The final prototype balances simplicity, safety, and fun—promoting real-world interaction through digital empowerment.

Design Challenge – Football & Hockey Club

Logo concept development

This fictional assignment was about finding the right visual expression for a club combining football and hockey. The focus was on choosing a font and defining a tone of voice that could support both sports – something active, clear, and accessible without feeling too loud or playful. I explored how typography and language could reflect team spirit and structure, while staying functional across digital platforms.
